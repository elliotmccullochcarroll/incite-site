Signal types

Every flag. Explained.

InCite watches for nine patterns where AI models are most likely to mislead. Here are three of the most common.

Citations

Authors and studies that may not exist.

AI models can fabricate plausible-sounding academic citations — correct journal format, realistic author names, real-looking DOIs — that point to papers that were never written. InCite flags any sentence with a named study, author, or journal.

Medical

Dosages you shouldn't copy without checking.

Specific drug names, dosages, titration schedules, and clinical protocols are high-stakes claims. Even when a model gets the general answer right, the precise figure may be wrong, outdated, or inappropriate for a specific patient profile.

Temporal

Facts that may have changed since training.

Time-anchored claims like "as of 2024" or "currently the leading…" can become silently wrong as the world moves on. The model's confidence doesn't degrade as its knowledge ages — InCite's temporal signal surfaces these for review.
